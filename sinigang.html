Fish Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is most often associated with tamarind in modern times, but it originally referred to any meat or seafood cooked in a sour broth,

Sinigang is best consumed during rainy days or cold weather, in general. Its hot and sour broth can help you feel warm and cozy.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs. salmo fillet.
  • 3 10g pouches Knorr Sinigang na Isda mix.
  • 1 bunch kangkong leaves removed and stalk cut into small pieces.
  • 10 pcs. string beans cut into to 2 inches length.
  • 2 medium ripe tomatoes sliced into wedges.
  • 6 to 8 pcs. lady finger(okra).
  • 6 ounces radish sliced.
  • 1 medium onion sliced.
  • 4 pcs. long green peppers.
  • 3 tablespoons patis.
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper.
  • 1 1/2 quart water.
